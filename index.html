Precision in Progress:

Edinburgh Instruments' Success Story with Celoxis

Edinburgh Instruments Ltd is a part of TechComp Europe, a global leader in the design, development, manufacture, and distribution of analytical and laboratory instruments. Their spectroscopic instruments are widely used for research and development, focusing on physical and chemical analysis.

Industry

Analytical and Laboratory Equipment Manufacturing

Location

Livingston, Scotland

The Challenge

Edinburgh Instruments, with its multiple research and development teams, faced significant challenges in managing projects and resources efficiently. They relied heavily on manual processes and Excel spreadsheets, which led to inefficiencies and difficulty in maintaining accurate records across teams. Specific challenges included:

Lack of a Formal Project Management Tool:

Project management was done on Excel, which wasn't designed for tracking complex, multi-phase projects.

Inefficient Resource Allocation

The absence of a centralized system meant that it was difficult to track the workload across different R&D teams and allocate resources effectively.

Time-Consuming Cost Calculations

Calculating the cost of projects, including employee time and resource allocation, was slow and prone to errors.

Communication Gaps with Global Stakeholders

Stakeholders located outside the UK faced difficulty accessing real-time project updates.

Limited Visibility for Senior Management

With no clear visualization of project progress or resource allocation, management struggled to make informed decisions.

Struggle faced before adopting Celoxis

  • Before adopting Celoxis, Edinburgh Instruments' project management process was disjointed and inefficient.

  • Excel, while useful for basic data entry, proved inadequate for managing their diverse portfolio of projects.

  • Team collaboration was hindered, especially when it came to project cost estimation, resource planning, and communication with global stakeholders.

  • Without a unified platform, ensuring , and the leadership had little insight into ongoing projects or employee workloads.

  • Additionally, project reporting was time-consuming, making it difficult for the team to track progress, calculate costs, and stay on top of deadlines.

What Changed After Celoxis?

After implementing Celoxis, Edinburgh Instruments experienced transformative changes in how they managed projects and resources. The comprehensive project management software enabled them to streamline various processes and improve efficiency across the board.

Increased Transparency

Celoxis allowed all stakeholders, including those outside the UK, to access real-time project updates at any time, improving visibility and decision-making. This led to a 30% improvement in transparency, making project tracking more accessible for everyone involved.

Efficient Project and Resource Planning

With Celoxis' project planning and workload charts, R&D teams could now see their tasks weeks or even months ahead, enabling better resource allocation and forecasting. As a result, the company saw a 25% reduction in time spent on resource planning, allowing teams to focus more on core tasks.

Accurate Cost Reporting

Celoxis' time-sheeting and cost reporting functions made it much easier for the company to track and calculate project expenses. This shift provided more accurate budget projections, with cost estimations becoming 15% more precise, resulting in better budget management across projects.

Improved Decision-Making for Senior Management

Senior leadership gained detailed insights into resource utilization, allowing them to make informed recruitment decisions when teams were overstretched. The system's ability to highlight overloaded resources also contributed to quicker decision-making in terms of hiring and workload distribution.

Enhanced Product Launch Planning

With better project planning tools, product managers could now estimate launch timelines more accurately, ensuring smoother product releases and increased visibility into project delivery timelines.
